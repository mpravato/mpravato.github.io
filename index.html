I see you've found me. Uh oh.

Morgan is a rising senior at the University of Maryland, College Park pursuing a double degree in journalism (broadcast) and dance. Besides being a student, she is an entertainment journalist, digital creator, dancer, and educator. She has a passion for storytelling through multimedia and movement.

How many college students use those labels and adjectives on job applications and in cover letters though?

Boring.

Let’s get a bit deeper. Here’s who she is, how she got here, and where she wants to go.

Ok, we're going to ditch this third-person nonsense because I absolutely cannot take myself seriously enough to keep it going.

My path has been messy so far, but I thrive in the chaos. To be honest, I live the dream by doing what I love inside and outside the classroom everyday. I cannot imagine staying cooped up in the library to study for a class that I am not absolutely in love with with. I live for the feeling of getting so lost in my work in the dance studio or in the news bubble that I don't realize it's 1 a.m.

When I was applying to colleges nearly four years ago, I never questioned whether I was going to balance my interest in journalism with my love for dance. It was simply a matter of how I was going to manage working behind a camera and on a stage. Now, I know that pursuing passions that were not obviously compatible has pushed the boundaries of my creativity - and my time management skills of course. Arts and entertainment journalism is not taken seriously by everyone in Merrill College, so I made my own creative niche by tailoring coursework and employment opportunities to appeal to both my hard reporting and creative sides. Now, I am especially proud to have a resume that boasts truly unique experiences. My work life may seem "all over the map" at first, but my urge to create connects it all. It is obvious that I can deliver in any situation or for any assignment thrown at me, while always providing a unique perspective and a bit of humor.

Multimedia

As a journalist focued on entertainment, culture, and the arts, I balance serious and influential reporting with comical creativity. I tell the stories of student artists and reflect on the impact that Jersey Shore had on my generation all in the same week for the same publication. I am fueled equally by my love for the arts and my stupid addiction to reality tv, TikTok, and other societal scum. I switch between these "sides" with ease, which means some of my stories have had important impacts and others simply made people laugh. I write for college students on the daily, but I am no stranger to audiences with more serious expectations. My dynamic voice makes me fit to create any digital content for virtually any audience.

While I still love reporting, I continue to establish myself in the world of digital communications and marketing. I had full creative control over two social media accounts for different University of Maryland academic departments. I was challenged to put forward professional content that was still attractive to college students and appropriate for the platforms. I redesigned their unattractive academic messages by planning, shooting, and editing my own photos/graphics/videos. I even created a few memes along the way. I rapidly grew both accounts' audiences and engagement rates.

I continue to strengthen both sides of my multimedia work. I am just as excited about arts advocacy as I am about showing how TikTok will be the next SoundCloud.

Dance

I began dancing at three years old, mainly training in modern styles, ballet, and tap. I do not feel like myself if I cannot move and groove every day. While an undergraduate student, I have worked under the mentorship of Adriane Fang.

Since dance has had such an important impact on my life, I obviously use my writing and video skills to more thoroughly cover the arts in the DMV area. I value the educational power of my work and have pursued additional movement-related opportunities to continue sharing the power of the arts. During the summer of 2019, I applied my training with Jody Arnold and Anne Biddle at the Dance Education Laboratory to singlehandedly create and implement a day camp’s first performing arts program. Creating a drama-dance hybrid curriculum for children ranging in age from 3-13 was one of my most rewarding professional experiences. Seeing the power that daily artistic practice, a welcoming environment, and a dose of laughter had on children re-inspired all aspects of my work.

I move forward in my career knowing that I am the product of an odd combination of passions, which allows me to be truly open to anything. At my core, I am an entertainer - I always hope to engage an audience whether that is through words or visuals onstage / on screen. I realize that's just a fancy way of saying that I like attention. That may be true too.

Interested in seeing more? Hopefully. This is only an overview of my passions and motivations. Check out my resume for more information about my experiences and skills. See examples of my multimedia and dance work too. Enjoy your stay, and stay connected once you leave.
